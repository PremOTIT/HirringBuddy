🧸 the basics

What this is

HiringBuddy has two parts. You already have the app on your phone — the dashboards you screen-share in interviews. This is the second part: a little helper that lives inside Telegram, made for the day-to-day work of scheduling interviews, logging candidates on the go, and getting reminded before things happen.

You don't need to choose between them. Think of the bot as the quick, conversational way in — perfect for when you're between meetings and just want to jot something down without opening a whole app.

🐝 step one

Getting started

1

Open the link you were sent

Tapping it opens a chat with the bot directly, the same way any Telegram contact would.

2

Say hello

Send /start — this is the only "setup" step. The bot introduces itself and shows everything it can do.

3

Just talk to it like texting a colleague

No menus to hunt through — everything happens through short messages, the same way you'd message anyone else.

📝 step two

Adding a candidate

This is the thing you'll do most — and the bot walks you through it step by step, asking one question at a time.

1

Send /add

The bot asks for the candidate's name first.

2

Answer each question as it comes

Name → Role → Sourcing channel → Current stage → Recruiter → Email. Send '-' to skip anything optional.

3

You'll get a confirmation with their number

Something like "✅ Added Ananya — candidate #1." That number is how you'll refer to them from now on.

💡 Tip: Fields like email and recruiter are optional — skip them with '-' if you don't have that info yet. You can always add it later.
👤 step three

Seeing everything about one candidate

Once you have a candidate's number, /profile pulls together everything known about them — role, stage, scorecard, and full interview history — into one message.

/profile 3

If they haven't been scored or scheduled yet, the bot tells you exactly what to do next instead of showing an empty screen.

🎯 step four

Scoring an interview

Right after speaking with someone, /score walks you through five quick questions — Technical depth, Communication, Problem solving, Time management, Collaboration — each rated 1 to 5.

/score 3

Just send a number each time it asks. Takes under a minute, saved permanently against that candidate.

📅 step five

Scheduling an interview

One line books an interview. The round number is tracked automatically — you never have to remember or type it yourself.

/schedule 3 2026-07-15 14:30 Technical @Aditya Group

Candidate #3, July 15th at 2:30 PM, a Technical round, for Aditya Group. Company name is optional.

⏰ Note: You'll automatically get a reminder shortly before this interview starts — no need to set anything extra.
✅ step six

Logging how it went

Right after a call, one short message closes the loop.

/outcome 5 Hired Great structured thinking, clear communicator

Outcome must be one of: Hired, Rejected, No-show, or Pending. Everything after that is a free-text note, entirely optional.

☀️ step seven

Your morning digest

Every morning, without asking, the bot sends one message summarizing the day ahead — every interview scheduled for today, who they're with, and what round it is.

☀️ This lands on its own, around 8:00 AM. You never need to check for it — it just arrives, so your day starts already informed.
📋 reference

Full command reference

You never need to memorize any of this — the bot always tells you the right command at the right moment.

CommandWhat it does
/addLog a new candidate, step by step
/listSee your recent candidates
/profile <id>Everything about one candidate
/score <id>Add or update an interview scorecard
/stage <id> <stage>Move a candidate to a new stage
/dashboardA quick text summary of your live metrics
/delete <id>Remove a candidate
/schedule <id> <date> <time>Book an interview — round tracked automatically
/pipelineSee upcoming interviews ("all" for full history)
/todayWhat's on today, right now
/outcome <id> <result>Log Hired, Rejected, No-show, or Pending
/reschedule <id>Move an interview to a new time
/cancelinterview <id>Remove a scheduled interview
/startSay hello — shows everything the bot can do
/cancelStep out of whatever you're mid-way through

5

💬 last thing

A few honest notes

  • This bot is private — only approved people can use it, so your data stays yours.
  • The bot and the HiringBuddy app are two separate tools right now. A candidate added in one won't automatically appear in the other — pick whichever feels natural for each moment.
  • If something ever goes wrong, the bot tells you plainly what happened instead of staying silent.
  • Nothing here is permanent except deleting a candidate, and even that requires you to type the command deliberately.
